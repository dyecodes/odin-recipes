Chinese Orange Chicken

Description

If you love asian take-out, this recipe is for you! Chinese Orange Chicken is a recipe I found online that is "way better than take-out"—according to the original author. My girlfriend and I love this recipe—and we can confirm that it is a cost-effective and tasty replacement for take-out. The quality will probably be better than—or at least on par with—most chinese take-out (at least in the midwestern US). Waay better than the frozen grocery store option.

This recipe was already pretty great, but I've adjusted a few minor things, including the cornstarch/water ratio. The chicken does take a bit of preparation, but otherwise this dish pretty straightforward to make. If you have a rice cooker, it's even easier! If you don't already have a rice cooker, do yourself a favor and go buy one. Seriously.

Ingredients

  • Chicken:
    • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 3 eggs, whisked
    • 1/2 cup cornstarch
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • Peanut oil (for frying)
    • MSG or salt
  • Orange Chicken Sauce:
    • 1 cup orange juice
    • 1/2 cup white sugar
    • 2 tbsp. rice vinegar
    • 2 tbsp. soy sauce
    • 1/4 tsp. ginger, minced
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1/2 tsp. red chili flakes
    • Orange zest from 1 orange
    • 2 tbsp. cornstarch
  • Garnish (optional):
    • Green onions
    • Orange zest
    • Red chili flakes
  • Rice:
    • 3 cups dry Calrose rice (I use Botan brand)

Steps

  1. To begin cooking rice:
  2. Rinse rice 4-7 times, or until water starts to run more clear.
  3. Add rice and 4 cups water to rice cooker. Begin cooking on regular "white rice" setting.
  4. To make orange sauce:
  5. In a medium pot, add orange juice, sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and red chili flakes. Heat for 3 minutes.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk 2 Tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 Tablespoons of water to form a paste. Add to orange sauce and whisk together. Continue to cook for 5 minutes, until the mixture begins to thicken. Once the sauce is thickened, remove from heat and add orange zest. Save some orange zest for garnish, if desired.
  7. To make chicken:
  8. Place flour and cornstarch in a shallow dish or pie plate. Add a generous pinch of MSG/salt. Stir.
  9. Whisk eggs in shallow dish.
  10. Dip chicken pieces in flour mixture, then in egg mixture, and then again in flour mixture. Place on plate.
  11. Heat 2 -3 inches of oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Using a thermometer, watch for it to reach 350 degrees.
  12. Working in batches, cook several chicken pieces at a time. Cook for 2 - 3 minutes, turning often until golden brown. Place chicken on a paper-towel-lined plate. Repeat (yes, you need to fry the chicken twice). Be careful to watch the time carefully and avoid overcooking.
  13. Toss chicken with orange sauce. Reserve some of the sauce to place on the rice, if desired.
  14. To bring it all together and serve:
  15. Serve chicken on rice with a sprinkling of green onion and orange zest, if desired. I like to put some extra red chili flakes on mine as well. Enjoy!
